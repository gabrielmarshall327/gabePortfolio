Aggies Create is a student led organization I am part of that works with various companies and clients to create a solution. While gaining professional experience by working for companies, this is also giving back to people by coming up with free solutions. Currently, I am working a AI medical wristband for a company called Nurowerx and previously was working with a start-up company to create a smart plant pot by designing and creating a mobile application that connected to the plant pot.

The Big Event is a massive volunteering opportunity available to Texas A&M students with the sole purpose of giving back to the Bryan/College Station community. This is an annual event that averages almost 15,000 student volunteers. It has become one of the biggest one-day, student-ran events in the world while being done by the best university. The work is usually yard work, painting, or possibly building.

Aggieland Humane Society is a non-profit animal shelter in Bryan/College Station. The shelter takes in almost any kind of animals, ranging from dogs and cats to pigs and chickens. This was started during my freshmen year of high school since volunteering was a strict requirement to graduate. However, I still volunteer there today to see all the new animals that arrive and hopefully get to adopt one someday!

Aggie Coding Club is another student led organization that is focused on teaching and creating coding projects. The organization will take anyone, no matter how experienced with coding. For example, during my sophomore year, I was able to be part of the Build-a-Rocket team and now mentor for that project. I was also part of creating a mobile app, Paw, my freshmen year which thought me a ton of full-stack development. There are also various workshops held, ranging from web development, AI, game development, discord bots and much more.
